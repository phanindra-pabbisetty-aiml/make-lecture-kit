Introduction

How Computers Handle Language

We will explore two sides of Natural Language Processing: Understanding (NLU) and Generation (NLG).

NLU (Understanding)

Taking raw, unstructured text and turning it into structured meaning that a computer can act upon.

NLG (Generation)

Taking structured data or concepts and turning them into fluent, human-readable text.

Natural Language Understanding

Reading Between the Lines

When a human reads a sentence, they instantly grasp the meaning. When a computer reads a sentence, it just sees a string of letters.

To understand text, a computer uses a pipeline. It breaks the text into tokens (words), identifies the intent (what the user wants), and extracts entities (important details).

Natural Language Generation

Writing the Story

NLG is the opposite of NLU. It is the process of taking structured data and turning it into plain, readable human language.
